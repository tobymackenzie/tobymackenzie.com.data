I've discontinued my Amazon Prime membership, opting for just the regular account.<!--more-->  They've increased the price over the years, and though they added features, I don't really use most of them.  The Prime Video has some good stuff but it's a pretty small selection overall, with things coming and going and it rarely has what I'm feeling like at the moment.  I still get free shipping as a non-member.  It is perhaps a little slower, but still plenty fast for me.  I have to hit a $35 minimum to get free shipping, but I was already grouping my purchases as a holdover from when I didn't have Prime.

And of course, Amazon the company is getting a bit too successful and powerful to give extra money to.  They seem to be aggressive about pushing Prime too.  I set my account to not auto-renew last year.  As my end date approached, they sent an email saying that my account would renew soon.  So much for disabled auto-renew.  Then they sent a text saying the same thing.  That was enough to make me feel certain in my decision to leave.  So I logged in, found where to quit, was briefly stopped by the emphasis on pausing instead of cancelling, but ultimately went forward with fully cancelling.  I can rejoin at any time if I want.
